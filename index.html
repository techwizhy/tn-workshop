OUR MISSION

Who We Are

The Tamil Nadu Youth Political Workshop is an initiative of the Government of Tamil Nadu, designed to bridge the gap between passionate young citizens and meaningful civic participation. We demystify the political system — from Gram Panchayat to State Secretariat — and equip participants with real, practical tools to enter public life with confidence, ethics, and purpose.

GENDER INCLUSION

Why We Champion Women in Politics

Tamil Nadu has been a national leader in women's political representation — reserving 50% of local body seats for women. Yet reserved seats alone cannot build true leadership. Our programme provides specialised training, confident public speaking skills, and a network of mentors who are themselves elected women leaders.

  • ✅ Women-only batches with safe, supportive learning environments
  • ✅ Mentorship from serving women MLAs and Panchayat Presidents
  • ✅ Training on reserved constituency strategies and election law
  • ✅ Self-confidence, public speaking, and media-handling modules

QUESTIONS

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can apply for the Tamil Nadu Youth Political Workshop?

The workshop is open to Indian citizens between 18 and 35 years of age who are residents of Tamil Nadu. There are no educational restrictions — we welcome participants from all backgrounds. Special women-only batches are available for female applicants seeking a safer, focused learning environment. Candidates from Economically Weaker Sections (EWS) are encouraged to apply, as subsidised and free seats are available.

Is the workshop free or paid?

The workshop is heavily subsidised by the Government of Tamil Nadu. For Economically Weaker Section (EWS) applicants, seats are available at no cost. A nominal fee may apply for other categories to cover training materials and certification. Complete fee details will be communicated after your application is reviewed and shortlisted. No applicant is turned away due to financial constraints.

How long is the programme?

The core workshop runs for 6 to 8 months of intensive offline sessions. Sessions are typically held on weekends and selected weekdays to accommodate working youth. After completing the core workshop, participants may enrol in the long-term Political Leadership School, which offers continued mentorship, campaign internships, and advanced governance studies for an additional 6 months.

Will I receive a certificate at the end?

Yes. All participants who successfully complete the programme receive a Certificate of Completion endorsed by the Government of Tamil Nadu. This certificate is recognised by Tamil Nadu district administration offices and can be cited in political party membership applications, election affidavits, and civic leadership roles. An additional Certificate of Excellence is awarded to the top 10% of each batch.

Is this workshop aligned with any political party?

Absolutely not. The Tamil Nadu Youth Political Workshop is strictly non-partisan. The curriculum is rooted in constitutional values — democracy, justice, equality, secularism, and fraternity — not any party ideology. Participants from all political affiliations are welcome. The programme does not endorse or campaign for any party, and trainers are bound by a non-partisan code of conduct.

Will this programme help me contest elections?

Yes, significantly. The Advanced track (Months 3–5) specifically covers grassroots campaign management, voter outreach, booth management, volunteer coordination, and Election Commission of India compliance. You will learn from experienced campaign managers and study real Tamil Nadu constituency case studies. Graduates who wish to contest local body elections receive dedicated support in identifying appropriate seats and drafting their electoral strategy.

Is the programme conducted in Tamil or English?

The programme is fully bilingual — conducted in both Tamil and English. All training materials, handouts, policy documents, and digital resources are available in both languages. Tamil-medium participants are not disadvantaged in any module. Special Tamil-language groups are formed on request for participants who are not comfortable in English.

What documents do I need to apply?

The following documents will be required at the time of in-person verification after shortlisting: (1) Aadhaar Card — for age and identity verification; (2) Recent passport-size photograph; (3) Address proof — voter ID, ration card, or utility bill; (4) Educational qualification certificate — any level; (5) Caste certificate (optional, for EWS fee waiver); (6) For women-only batches — no additional documents required, gender declaration in the form is sufficient.

Are there special opportunities for women participants?

Yes — we strongly encourage women to apply. There are dedicated women-only batches (such as Batch 10 in Chennai) with a safe, supportive learning environment. Women participants receive mentorship from serving women MLAs and Panchayat Presidents. Specific modules cover the 73rd and 74th Constitutional Amendments on reserved seats, election law for women candidates, self-confidence and public speaking, and strategies for winning in reserved constituencies. Over 1,000 women leaders have been mentored through this programme so far.

How can I donate, and will I receive an 80G tax exemption receipt?

You can donate through the Donation section of this page using UPI (scan the QR code or use the UPI ID workshop@tngovt), Debit/Credit Card, Net Banking, or NEFT/RTGS bank transfer. The minimum donation is ₹101. To receive a tax exemption receipt under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act, please provide your PAN card number at the time of donation. An official receipt will be emailed to you within 48 hours of successful payment. Please consult your tax advisor to confirm eligibility.
